# Platform-Specific Ad Rules

## How to Use
Read the relevant platform section when generating ads. Present rules as
recommendations to the user — they can override via element selection checkboxes.

---

## Meta (Facebook + Instagram)

### General
- Text coverage: aim for <20% of image area (Meta no longer hard-blocks but
  algorithmically deprioritizes text-heavy ads — lower reach, higher CPMs)
- Recommended on-visual elements: headline (max 5 words), CTA button, logo,
  regulatory disclaimer
- Move to ad caption: support lines, bullet points, trust signals, offer details

### Feed — 1:1 (1080×1080) / 4:3 (1200×900)
- Reduced layout: headline + hero subject + CTA + logo
- Single focal point — no split compositions or collage layouts
- CTA button: ~40% frame width, bottom-center
- Logo: top-right corner, 2-line logotype

### Story/Reel — 9:16 (1080×1920)
- Safe zones: top ~14% (status bar/profile), bottom ~20% (CTA overlay/swipe-up)
- Keep headline and CTA in the middle 66% vertical band
- Can include ONE additional support line if space allows
- CTA button: ~60% frame width

## LinkedIn

### General
- More text-tolerant than Meta — professional audience reads more
- Data points and stats on image perform well (e.g., "100,000+ SMEs funded")
- Recommended on-visual: headline (max 7 words), CTA, logo, one stat/trust signal

### Feed — 1.91:1 (1200×627)
- Professional aesthetic — clean, structured layouts
- CTA button: solid brand color, clear contrast
- Logo: top-right corner
- Can include trust signal or data point near CTA

### Story — 9:16 (1080×1920)
- Same safe zones as Meta stories
- Professional tone maintained

---

## TikTok — 9:16 (1080×1920)

### General
- Visual-first platform — minimal text, let imagery speak
- Native/authentic feel outperforms overly polished ads
- Recommended on-visual: headline (max 4 words), CTA button, logo

### Safe Zones
- Right side: ~15% reserved for interaction buttons (like, comment, share)
- Bottom: ~25% reserved for caption, music, username
- Top: ~10% reserved for status bar
- Keep all critical elements in center-left area

---

## Google Display

### Leaderboard — 728×90 (extreme horizontal)
- Headline + CTA only — extremely compact
- Logo: left side, small
- CTA: right side, prominent
- No body copy — won't fit legibly

### Medium Rectangle — 300×250
- Headline + CTA + logo
- Can include one product image
- Border recommended (1px) for visibility on white backgrounds

### Wide Skyscraper — 160×600 (extreme vertical)
- Vertical text stack: logo top → headline → image → CTA bottom
- Very limited horizontal space — 2-3 words per line max

### General
- High contrast required — ads appear on varied website backgrounds
- Clear brand identification (logo visible at small sizes)
- CTA must be visually distinct and clickable-looking

---

## YouTube — 16:9 (1280×720)

### Thumbnail
- Face or product should fill ~60% of frame
- Bold headline: max 4 words, large enough to read at thumbnail size
- High contrast colors — must stand out in YouTube's feed
- Avoid bottom-right corner (timestamp overlay)
- Logo: top-left or top-right, small

### Display Companion — 300×250
- Same rules as Google Display Medium Rectangle
