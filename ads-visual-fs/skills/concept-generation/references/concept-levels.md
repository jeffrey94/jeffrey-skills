# Concept Level Reference

## Level Controls

| Level | Name | Conceptual Distance | Image Strength | Risk |
|-------|------|---------------------|---------------|------|
| 1 | SAFE (REFRAME) | Minimal | 0.55–0.65 | Low |
| 2 | BOLD (TRANSFORM) | Moderate | 0.70–0.80 | Medium |
| 3 | EXPERIMENTAL (TRANSCEND) | High | 0.85–0.95 | High |

## Level 1 — SAFE (REFRAME)

**Strategy**: Take what works and make it better. Minimal creative risk.

**Approach**:
- Polish the existing visual language
- Improve copy clarity and impact
- Refine color balance within existing palette
- Enhance layout hierarchy and whitespace
- Upgrade photography/illustration quality

**Example**: An SME loan ad with a professional photo and clear CTA → same structure but with better typography, tighter copy, and more impactful photography.

## Level 2 — BOLD (TRANSFORM)

**Strategy**: Keep the product hero but change the storytelling approach.

**Approach**:
- New visual metaphor (e.g., growth → building blocks instead of charts)
- Rebuilt composition (different layout structure)
- Fresh color emphasis within brand palette
- New emotional angle (trust → empowerment, security → freedom)
- Product remains the central focus

**Example**: An SME loan ad showing funding stats → transformed to show an SME owner's journey from application to growth, with the product as the enabler.

## Level 3 — EXPERIMENTAL (TRANSCEND)

**Strategy**: Genre-shift. Only product identity and core message survive.

**Approach**:
- Unexpected visual language (e.g., abstract art, editorial photography, cinematic)
- Break category conventions
- New emotional territory
- Product recognition maintained but not necessarily hero
- Core message reframed in new context

**Example**: An SME loan ad → editorial-style image of a night market owner at golden hour, no traditional ad layout, just the tagline and a subtle CTA — feels like a magazine cover.
